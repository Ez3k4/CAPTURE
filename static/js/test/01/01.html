Antibiotic Resistance

When comparing the leading causes of death with the number of annual deaths caused by antimicrobial resistance you will find numbers around 0.7 M in the literature.
And that's without counting the cases that are indirectly linked to AMR or were simply filed under a different cause of death.
But estimates predict that by the year 2050, this number will have risen to about the same magnitude as the number of annual deaths caused by cancer.
This means that antimicrobial resistance already is a significant issue and will become an even bigger concern in the near future!

AMP - the alternative to Antibiotics

But what exactly are AMPs?
Antimicrobial peptides or AMPs are short cationic peptides with a length of around 50 amino acids.
They're produced by the innate immune system of many organisms and combat bacteria through various mechanisms.
Now let's take a look how the AMP could be safely delivered and produced inside antibiotic resistent bacteria like Pseudomonas aeruginosa

The Plasmid

For biosafety reasons, we've incorporated a constitutive Pseudomonas-specific promoter called pel into the plasmid.
This design guarantees that even if the plasmid is taken up by a lung epithelial cell, the AMPs wont be expressed.

Delivery System

We developed two different delivery system where the Plasmid gets encapsulated by lipids to transport it into the bacteria.
These transport vesicles only target specified bacteria strains like Pseudomonas aeruginosa.

Pseudomonas aeruginosa

Pseudomonas aeruginosa is recognized by the WHO as a high priority pathogen, with urgent need for new therapeutics due to its extremely effective mechanisms for antibiotic resistance and the complex and life threatnig host responses it triggers.
